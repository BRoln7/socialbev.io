Social-BEV: Learning Socially Compliant Robot Navigation via Intention-Aware Scene Representations

Abstract

Socially compliant navigation is essential for robots operating in human-populated environments, requiring them to proactively respond to human expectations. Currently, deep reinforcement learning (DRL) offers a promising approach for learning navigation policies that adhere to complex social norms. However, most of them rely on limited scene representations of the pedestrians' planar trajectory. They oversimplify critical social and environmental contexts, resulting in poor generalization to complex and dynamic scenarios in the real-world. To address this issue, Social-BEV, a novel intention-aware scene representation, is introduced to holistically encode social contexts from egocentric visual observations. Our method employs two key components: a spatio-temporal encoder for extracting Bird’s-Eye View (BEV) features, and Intent-Interact Former (I$^2$Former) that infers pedestrian intentions from 3D poses to model human-robot interactions. These features are unified into a compact state embedding that supports effective DRL policy training. Extensive experiments show that our method achieves superior performance over baselines, and real-world deployment demonstrates vision-based socially compliant navigation in unseen crowded scenarios.

System Overview

Our method consists of three primary components: a feature extraction module, a feature fusion module, and a DRL network. It takes multi-timestep RGB-D images, pedestrian poses, and the robot’s internal states as inputs. In the representation encoding stage, the spatio-temporal encoder and the I^2Former extract intention-aware scene representations, which are then fused with the robot’s state embedding to form DRL state embedding and fed into the DRL policy for navigation.
