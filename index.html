Social-BEV: Learning Robotic Socially Compliant Navigation via Intention-Aware Scene Representations

Han Bao1,*, Bingyi Xia1,2,*, Hanjing Ye1, Yu Zhan1, Hao Cheng1, Wenjun Xu2, Jiankun Wang1
1Southern University of Science and Technology
2Peng Cheng National Laboratory
*Indicates Equal Contribution
Code arXiv

Abstract

Effective social navigation requires the robot to develop a comprehensive understanding of the surrounding social context. However, existing methods overly simplify scene representations by reducing them to relative coordinates on a 2D plane. In this article, we propose Social-BEV, which generates intention-aware scene representations by compressing egocentric RGB-D inputs into unified state embeddings. It enhances Bird’s-Eye View (BEV) representations by extracting features of pedestrian poses, prioritizing the human-robot-environment interaction for policy learning. Simulation and real-world experiments validate that our task-oriented visual scene representations support zero-shot sim-to-real transfer and yield promising navigation performance in both point-to-point crowd navigation and long-distance human-aware navigation scenarios. Experiments demonstrate that our navigation policy achieves superior performance over baselines with respect to success rate, navigation efficiency, and pedestrian disturbance metrics.
